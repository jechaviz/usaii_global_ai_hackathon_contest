## Rule Notes Used

- Devpost lists registration through 2026-06-06, qualifier 2026-06-07 to
  2026-06-10, build period 2026-06-14 to 2026-06-21, and final submission
  deadline 2026-06-21 23:59 ET.
- Devpost requires all participants to be currently enrolled students, teams of
  2-5, individual Devpost registration, and highest education track selection.
- Devpost requires the qualifier before Phase 2 and a valid approval code for
  final submission.
- Devpost requires substantial work during the hackathon build window and
  prohibits pre-built solutions, plagiarism, unauthorized proprietary data, and
  harmful/discriminatory systems.
- Devpost final submission requirements include project description, track and
  challenge, AI architecture, human-in-loop design, responsible AI guardrails,
  tools/data disclosure, a 3-5 minute pitch video, and a working demo or
  walkthrough.
- USAII FAQ says the event is virtual/global, teams build prototypes and submit
  ideas, qualifier responses focus on AI thinking and responsible technology use,
  final submissions can use prototypes or demonstrations, synthetic/mock data is
  acceptable, and judging evaluates problem understanding, AI reasoning, design,
  impact, and responsible AI.
- USAII FAQ/how-it-works describe qualifier practice shape as a short scenario
  assessment with approximately 8 prompts, general themes of Health & Wellbeing,
  Sustainability, and Community, and no prototype required.
- USAII terms say teams that advance receive a unique qualifier code by email
  on 2026-06-11; Devpost lists qualifier results on 2026-06-12, so the runbook
  watches both dates.
