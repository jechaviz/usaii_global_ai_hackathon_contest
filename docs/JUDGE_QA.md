# Judge Q&A

## Is this just a chatbot?

No. The demo is an evidence-backed workflow. It scores fit, exposes skill gaps,
creates a portfolio plan, shows a rubric defense, and keeps risky actions behind
human approval.

## Why is AI needed?

The AI value is not generating motivational advice. It is turning messy student
intent, constraints, skills, opportunity requirements and rubric criteria into a
structured decision with traceable reasons.

## How do you know it works?

The current synthetic experiment compares generic advice against the coach in
five scenarios. Baseline average is 57, coach average is 86, decision delta is
+29, and risk flags fall by 71%.

## How do you prevent bias?

The public demo avoids protected-trait inference and uses synthetic profiles.
The product scores only stated goals, constraints, skills and opportunity
requirements. It shows missing evidence rather than hiding uncertainty.

## What happens if the recommendation is wrong?

The coach treats recommendations as decision support, not guarantees. The
student can inspect reasons, adjust skills, challenge gaps, and approve or
reject outreach manually.

## What data do you use?

Public demo data is synthetic. During the hackathon, opt-in student feedback can
be used only with consent and redaction. Enrollment, age, guardian and account
proof remain private.

## What would you build next?

- Live LLM preference extraction with deterministic rubric verification.
- A mentor review mode.
- Failure-mode examples and refusal receipts.
- Exportable portfolio rubric and opportunity shortlist.
